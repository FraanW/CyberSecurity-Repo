The app collects the password itself. Type your credentials into this app's own form below — no redirect to the IdP — and the app sends them to the token endpoint. That's the defining trait (and the risk) of ROPC.

When is ROPC actually OK? (the nuance)

    
    
      
  • ✅ Trusted first-party apps inside the org — legacy internal tools, CLIs, test automation, and migrations, where the app is fully owned/trusted and MFA isn't required for that path.
    • 
      
  • ❌ Never third-party or public apps — handing your password to someone else's app is exactly what OAuth exists to prevent.
    • 
      
  • ⚠️ The costs: the app sees the raw password, and there's no place for MFA, passkeys, or a consent screen in the flow.
    • 
      
  • 📜 Deprecated by the OAuth Security BCP (RFC 9700) and removed in OAuth 2.1 — but still common on internal, trusted systems. Prefer Auth Code + PKCE when you can.
