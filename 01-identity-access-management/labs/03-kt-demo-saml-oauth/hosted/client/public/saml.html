SAML 2.0 — SSO round trip

← back  ·  capture this with the SAML-tracer extension.

The story: a second application (realm finco-app) doesn't manage passwords — when you log in, it federates over SAML to the corporate IdP (realm finco-idp), which returns a signed assertion. Textbook SP-initiated SSO.

Before you click — arm the capture
  1. Click the SAML-tracer icon so its window is recording.
  2. Then click the button below and log in as farhaan / Passw0rd!.
  3. First time only: you may see a one-time "review profile" page — that's just-in-time provisioning on first federated login. Confirm it.

In SAML-tracer, find the two SAML rows and read them
SAMLRequestthe AuthnRequest (finco-app → finco-idp) — small, deflated+base64, unreadable by eye.
SAMLResponsethe signed assertion. Read: Issuer → Signature → NameID → Conditions (NotBefore/NotOnOrAfter) → Audience → AttributeStatement (email, givenName, surname, Role).

Punchline: the app never saw your password — it trusted a signed assertion. That signature is the whole security model.
