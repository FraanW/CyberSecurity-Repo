Two ways SAML SSO can start. Arm SAML-tracer before clicking, then log in as farhaan / Passw0rd!.

① SP-initiated SSO — you start at the App

The app has no session, so it builds a SAML AuthnRequest and redirects you to the IdP. The IdP authenticates you and POSTs a signed assertion back to the app's ACS. This is the everyday flow (~95% of SAML).

In SAML-tracer you'll see a SAMLRequest (AuthnRequest) then a SAMLResponse with InResponseTo tying them together.

② IdP-initiated SSO — you start at the IdP

You begin at the IdP (e.g. a portal tile). It sends the app an unsolicited assertion — no AuthnRequest, no InResponseTo. Convenient, but weaker (easier to replay), so modern practice prefers SP-init.

In SAML-tracer you'll see a SAMLResponse with no preceding SAMLRequest and no InResponseTo. That difference IS the distinction.
